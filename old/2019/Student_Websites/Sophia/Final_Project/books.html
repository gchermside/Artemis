Little Women

Little Women by Louisa May Alcott. This heartful coming of age novel, takes place in the 1860s. The story follows four sisters and their adventures of encountering love, loss, friendship and life changing descions. Alcott focuses the story on the four sisters Jo, Meg, Amy and Beth who are all very similar in position but differnet in character. Meg, the oldest March sister, battles her girlish weakness for luxury and money, and ends up marrying a poor man she loves. She has many motherlike qualities, that allow her to help her sisters and try and maintain a humble household when all things seem to fall apart among her sisters. Jo March is the main character of Little Women, Jo is an outspoken tomboy with a passion for writing, this passion carries her throughout the book even when all things seem to be lost. In the very beginning of the book Jo befriends her neighbor, Laurie, who later becomes very fond of Jo, too fond for her liking. One of Jo's important roles is taking care of her younger, ill and shy sister, Beth. Although Beth is perceived as a weak character she is strong in heart and her love for piano brings her joy. Amy, the youngest of the March sisters is the opposite of quiet, she is outgoing and is never afraid to speak her mind or do as she pleases. Which doesn't always work for her. While reading this book, you will experience ups and downs, love and heartbreak, triumphs and losses with the March sisters. I hope this brief summary of Little Women brings you to read this amazing book!

The Fault in our Stars

Many of you may have heard of this heartfelt love story by John Green. The Fault in our Stars is a present day realistic fiction novel. The main character Hazel who is 16, is constantly reluctant to go to her cancer support group. Hazel has diagnosed with lung cancer when she was 13. When Hazel meets a teenager named Augustus Waters who had osteosacroma, which is a rare bone cancer, but has been given the all clear. Hazel and Augustus instantly become friends and imbark on a variety of emotional adventures, including love, loss, and romance, while together they search for answers from their favorite author. Together they travel to Amsterdam to find Peter Van Houten, their favorite author and writer of An Imperial Infliction.This trip ends with the two friends in love with an other, but when heartbreaking news was told, both Hazel and Augustus's worlds fall apart around them and they only have eachother. During this rollercoaster ride of a book, you should expect to laugh, cry, smile and fall in love with this amzing book by John Green.
