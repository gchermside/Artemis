Food

Although Rhode Island is a small state, and you can practically drive all throughout the state in the span of one day, there are many great restuarants, cafes and other places to get all sorts of food. Listed down below with some description, are a few of my favorite places to eat in Providence and places I know many other people enjoy!

Places to Eat!

Denden

Dended Cafe' Asiana is located on 161 Benefit St, PVD, RI. At Denden you can enjoy hearty Korean disges such as hot-stone bibimbap, seafood pancakes, short rib BBQ, fried noodle rolls and many more. They also provide Japanese Soba, Rice bowls, and deep fried 'Katsu' specialties. Many people say that Denden has a welcoming vibe, and a livley city like scenary. When I go to Denden I always order the same thing, but I know other people who love a variety of the things on the menu, such as the bento boxes, and the bibimap. I order Edamame and the fried noddle rolls as appetizers, these two appetizers are great to share! As my entre I get the Chicken Katsu, which is a deep fried chicken cutlet, that comes with salad, a bowl of miso soup and a bowl of white rice. Denden is a great place to go with your family, it is kid friendly and the food is excellent!

Al Forno

Al Forno's is located on 577 S Water St, PVD, RI. Al Forno is an Italian restuarant with great food and the ombiance adds to the experience. Al Forno is known for their Grilled Pizzas, Clams Al Forno, amazing pasta dishes, and made-to-order desserts such as Hand-Churned Ice Cream, Fresh Fruit Tarts, and the Grand Cookie Finale. At my most recent visit to Al Forno, I shared the Pizza Margherita with my dad, and for my entre I had the pasta bolognese. As a dessert I normally have the flourless double chocolate cake, but as strawberries were in season they had a new edition to the dessert menu, Strawberry Shortcake with fresh wipped cream and fresh strawberries. It was the best strawberry shortcake I have ever had, the wipped cream wasn't too sweat, but the strawberries gave the sweetness and the shortcake has slightly hard on the outside, but soft and crumbly on in the outside. Al Forno's is my favorite restuarant in Providence, and the food consistantly meets your expectations!

Flatbreads

Flatbread Company is located on 161 Cushing St, PVD, RI (off of Thayer St). Flatbreads is a livley restuarant that is great for the family. Their pizzas are excellent, I usually get the no boundaries salad where you can add to their organic salad. I add goat cheese, carrots, and corn but it is the salad dressing that makes the salad great, they use their homemade ginger-tamari vinaigrette. Along with their tasty sald comes their wood fired pizza, and there are a variety to choose from and ones you can add toppings to. My favorite pizza is the Jay's heart, which is their wood-fired cauldron tomato sauce, premium whole milk mozzarella and parmesan cheese baked on organic bread dough with garlic oil and our blend of organic herbs. Flatbreads is a great place to go with any size party, and it is completley kid friendly!

Seven Stars

Seven Stars has a few locations throughout Rhode Island. I usually go to the one located on 820 Hope st, PVD, RI. Seven Stars is a buzzing nakery featuring from-scratch breads and pastries plus sandwhiches and carefully sourced coffee. My family and I go very often for their variety of breads, tasty sandwhiches which are great for lunch, and their variety of pastries that are great for breakfast, snack or bringing a dessert to a party! My favorite things at Seven stars include their arnold palmers, the ham and cheese sandwhich or their roasted turkey sandwhich, and their croissants, cookies, muffins and other pastries are delicous. This lively bakery is a great place to go and work, or go with your friends and family!

Three Sisters

Three Sisters is located on 1074 Hope st, PVD, RI. Three Sisters has a friendly atmosphere, that is great for kids and there is a park across the street. You can order breakfast food, or lunch/dinner food. But, what they are most known for is their amazing homemade ice cream and their variety of flavors. Their flavors include: kulfi, dutch chocolate, strawberry, vanilla. black raspberry, purple cow, green tea, dirty garden mint, and many many more! My favorite flavor is Dirty Garden Mint, which tastes as if you are eating real mint, and there are chocolate chips in it! Along with delicous ice cream, there are amazing cones to go with it! They have the typical cones, wafer and sugar, but they also have homemade waffle cones! Three Sisters is a one of a kind place, and they have some of the best ice cream I have ever had!

Knead Doughnuts

Knead Doughnuts has a variety of locations throughout Providence, 32 Custom House st, 55 Cromwell st, and 4230, 135 Elmgrove Ave. Knead Doughnuts in historic Providence crafts seasonally inspired and traditional gourmet donuts made with locally-sourced, wholesome ingredients. Although there are a variety of flavors of donuts, they have four main categories: old fashioned, cake, brioche and filled. In the old fashioned category there is: vanilla old fashioned, chocolate chip old fashioned and blueberry lemon basil. In the cake category there is: chocolate cake, cookies and cream, and strawberry shortcake. In the brioche category there is: vanilla galzed, cinnamon roll, passion fruit, brown butter pecan and strawberry glazed. Last but not least there is the filled category, which includes: Peanut butter and Jelly, jelly, and boston cream. Along with all these flavors there are seasonal flavors! Knead doughnuts has a lively scene that is great to sit and enjoy a tasty doughnut in their variety of locations!

Rebelle Bagels

Rebelle Artisan Bagels is located on 110 Doyle Ave, PVD, RI. Rebelle bagels are hand-rolled and boiled bagles in Providence. Rebelle is always lively and buzzing with people getting their bagels and pastries. From when they open till when they close they are serving warm welcomes and warm bagels. Their menu includes: plain, poppy, everything, sesame, onion, pretzel, pumoernickel, cinnamon raisin and their specials! Along with a tasty bagel, they have a variety of spreads, additions and even ways to make your bagel into a sandwhich! While you wait for your bagel you can enjoy a drink and a pastry! Rebelle is open from about 6:30 till they run out and they run out quick! So don't delay your bagel run because you don't want to miss out on these delicous bagels!
