In 2012, of the students in the eighth grade in Rhode Island, the expected percentage of students passing the mathematics NECAP was 66%. However, only 58.1% of the students passed the math NECAP. In 2013, the goal for the amount of passing grades for the mathematics NECAP was 71%. The percentage of students that actually passed was 57.3%.This means that more students are not meeting the state standards in math than expected, and the expected amount was generously low. We did a survey where we approached random people and asked them if they wanted to buy the app. Out of all of the people we surveyed, 100% agreed to buy our app. About 75% of them were college students, and the other 25% were in grade school. Despite the difference of academic levels and age, they all wanted this app. Although none of them felt that they struggled in math, they all agreed that extra math practice is very beneficial.
