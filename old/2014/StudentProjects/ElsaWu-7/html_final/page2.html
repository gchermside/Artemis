Relationships

Each character has his/her own likes and dislikes. Like all should. For example, England, and France fight like an old married couple. And America and Canada's relationship(like most of America's relationships) are onesided hate(not from America) and when they fight its hilarious to watch. But not all of everyone's relationships are of hate and bitterness.

Take Switzerland and Liechtenstien's brother and sister relationship for example. After Switerland saved the fellow Germanic nation from basically falling. She called him "Big brother"(which he kind of is, but you get the point), did nice things for him to try and pay him back for all the nice things he did for her. There are a few that I consider family relationships, Hungary, Austria, and child Italy's relationship, and Prussia and Hungary's relationship.

Things to keep in mind:

  • Do not think that Romano(South Italy) hates or is jealous of his little brother Italy(North Italy). He's just over protective and gives everyone tough love in public(but he can seem very rude to others).
  • Do not think that everyone doesn't get along with one another. They just dislike a few selective people, not everyone.
  • Make sure you understand that everything they do to others can't JUST be justified romatically. There are other ways to jusify an action.
