The Difference
  • In human languages a sentence can be syntactically correct and yet have no meaning.
  • That could never happen in a machine or mathematical language.
  • In machine or mathematical languages syntax dictates meaning.  In other words, anything with correct syntax has correct meaning.

Defining a Language
  •  Every sentence needs a noun phrase and a verb phrase.
  •  A noun phrase can be either
    •  A noun.
    •  Or an adjective followed by a noun.
  •  A verb phrase can be either
    •  A verb.
    •  Or a verb followed by an adverb.
