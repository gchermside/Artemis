1974
Brown women's crew established in 1974 under the direction of its first head coach, Peter Amram '61, a Brown graduate who had rowed with the men's varsity crew as an undergraduate.
Brown women victorious in their first intercollegiate meet, defeating Assumption College, Clark University, and Holy Cross on May 4, 1974.
1976
Two Brown rowers, Betts Howes '77 and Kathryn Reith '78, earned gold medals at the U.S. National Women's Rowing Championship in Long Beach, California, on June 20, 1976. Both women rowed in the open four and open eight races for the College Boat Club of the University of Pennsylvania.
1979
Gavin Viano named head coach of women's crew in 1979, following Amram's decision to leave the team to pursue other interests.
1980
Brown women's lightweight four finished first at the Eastern Sprints on May 11, 1980, marking their first ever Sprints title. The lightweight four went on to finish third in the College Nationals and sixth in the Open Nationals at the 1980 NWRA Women's Collegiate and National Rowing Championships held from June 18-22, 1980.
1983
In 1983, the second novice boat became the first Brown women's shell ever to qualify for the grand finals at the Eastern Sprints.
Four Brown women rowers, Ginny Golder '84, Tina Hartest '85, Anne Sobieski '85 and Alexa Lambert '85, compete in the National Championsips held at Madison, Wisconsin on June 4-5, 1983.
1984
John Murphy becomes the women's crew third head coach in 1984, coming to Brown after coaching stints at Cal Berkely and the University of Washington.
1988
The varsity eight capture the Women's Eastern Sprints Championship for the first time in Brown history in 1988.
Former Brown rower, Jennifer Corbet '87, competes for the United States in the 1988 Seoul Olympics.
1990
Brown women win first team title at the Eastern Sprints on May 13, 1990, as the second varsity and the first novice boats earn gold medals, the varsity four takes home silver, and the varsity eight and second novice boats win bronze medals.
1993
The Bears's varsity eight win the first women's race ever held at the Intercollegiate Rowing Association (IRA) Championships on June 5, 1993.
1994
The Bears repeat as IRA Champions in the second annual women's IRA Championship held on June 4, 1994. The women's crew had earlier finished second only to eventual national champion Princeton at the Eastern Sprints.
1995
Murphy coached the 1995 women's varsity eight to a bronze medal at the National Championship as well as a 5-1 regular season record and silver medal at Eastern Sprints.
1996
Brown's varsity eight decisively defeats two-time defending national champion Princeton during the 1996 regular season and goes on to win the 1996 Eastern Sprints, the IRAs, and the Collegiate National Championship, becoming the first women's crew to capture the "Triple Crown" of collegiate racing.
Former Brown rower, Samara Walbohm '93, competes for Canada in the 1996 Atlanta Olympics.
1997
Brown's varsity four wins the first NCAA Championship, narrowly defeating the University of Washington on Lake Natoma in Rancho Cordova, California on June 1, 1997. As a team, Brown finished third overall.
1998
In 1998, Brown places second at the NCAA Championship. The crew goes on to Henley and captures the open eight and club eight titles.
1999
Coach John Murphy's crew captures the first NCAA Division I Championship in Brown University history when the varsity pulls out a three second victory over the University of Virginia at the 1999 NCAA Championship on Lake Natoma in Rancho Cordova, California. The Bears' varsity also captured the Eastern Sprints Championship and Ivy Championship while setting a new course record. Brown's two freshman eights and varsity four also won at the Sprints, completing an undefeated season.
Coach John Murphy is voted the EAWRC Coach of the Year while the EAWRC Novice Coach of the Year is awarded to Coach Phoebe Murphy.
Brown rower and team captain, Rachel Anderson '00, wins a gold medal at the 1999 World Championships in the women's lightweight pair.
Brown rowers Portia Johnson '01 and Liane Malcos '00 win a bronze medal in a straight four at the Nations Cup.
