Using Variables
When you define a variable, the name comes after a quotation mark.  When you use a variable, however, the name comes after a colon.
Here is another example:
MAKE “num 360
REPEAT :num [FORWARD 1 RIGHT 1]
What do these commands do?
In this example, the first line defines the variable ‘bar’ to be the text “This is my text.”  The second line will print “This is my text.” to the window.
MAKE “bar [This is my text]
PRINT :bar
