What is the Artemis Project?

The Artemis Project is a five week summer program for girls finishing 8th grade, who are interested in science and technology.

Through activities such as e-mailing a pen pal and building an intelligent robot, students will be introduced to concrete computer skills and abstract computer science concepts. They will develop self-confidence and leadership in an encouraging and challenging environment.

When will the program be held?

The program will be held on the Brown University campus, from June 26 through July 28th, Monday through Friday. Students are expected to attend each day from 8:30 am to 3 pm. Transportation will be provided from Kennedy Plaza.

How will you learn?

  • Hands-on experience using computers in state-of-the-art facilities
  • Group problem solving activities
  • Interactive games
  • Building and programming Lego Mindstorm robots
  • Creating personal web pages
  • Guest lectures by Brown University professors
  • Field trips:
    • Brown Computer Science department
    • Virtual reality labs
    • Boston Museum of Science
  • E-mail with pen pals

What will you learn?

We begin by introducing the computer and its basic functions. You will learn the practical skills needed to use word processing, email, and web software. You'll learn how to write HTML (the language that web pages are written in), and make your very own web page that anyone can see.

We'll also focus on the use of logic and deductive reasoning while exploring topics in computer science. You'll use Legos(tm) to build robots, and program the robots to perform "intelligent" tasks. You'll also learn about the fields of artificial intelligence and computer animation, and learn about different ways of programming a computer.

By the end of this program, you will have the practical skills and conceptual bases needed to follow your curiosity and interests anywhere!

Who can apply?

Girls who will be entering the ninth grade in the Providence, R.I. public school system are encouraged to apply. If you are creative, motivated to learn and explore, and curious about math, science, or computers, then we would love to receive your application. No previous computer or engineering experience is necessary!

How do you apply?

If you do not have an application, you can download one here in PDF format. (This requires Acrobat Reader.) The application may be mailed or faxed to the address at the top of the form. Applications are due Monday, May 15, 2000.
