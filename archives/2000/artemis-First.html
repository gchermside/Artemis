What is the Artemis Project?

The Artemis Project is a free five week summer program for girls finishing 8th grade, who are interested in science and technology.

Through activities such as e-mailing a pen pal and building an intelligent robot, students will be introduced to concrete computer skills and abstract computer science concepts. They will develop self-confidence and leadership in an encouraging and challenging environment.

When will the program be held?

The program will be held on the Brown University campus, from June 26 through July 28th, Monday through Friday. Students are expected to attend each day from 8:30 am to 3 pm. Transportation will be provided from Kennedy Plaza.

How will you learn?

  • Hands-on experience using computers in state-of-the-art facilities
  • Group problem solving activities
  • Interactive games
  • Building and programming Lego Mindstorm robots
  • Creating personal web pages
  • Guest lectures by Brown University professors
  • Field trips:
    • Brown Computer Science department
    • Virtual reality labs
